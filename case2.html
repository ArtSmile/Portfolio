Scalable Platform Redesign + White-labeled Design System

Period Covered: Nov 2022 - Sep 2024 | Client: Johnson and Johnson | Role: Key Designer

Scalable Platform Redesign

A comprehensive redesign for one of the world's largest manufacturers of contact lenses. The goal was to create a cohesive, scalable platform that could seamlessly adapt to regional and cultural differences while delivering a unified experience.

Challenges

Modernizing 64 local websites, supporting 50 languages with varying writing and reading directions. Creating reusable components that could be efficiently implemented across all sites without sacrificing localized user experience.

Achievements

The redesign enhanced user experience across all markets, improved accessibility, and streamlined content management.

White-labeled Design System

The process of separation from the Consumer and is undertaking a significant technology transformation, which includes the Selection of the Next Generation Technology Platform aligned with MACH Architecture Principles that will become a foundation for massive migration efforts of more than 200 sites.

Challenges

Establishing a white-labeled design system and aligning several projects to ensure that all projects are consistent and meet the project objectives. Working closely with stakeholders, documenting the design system, maintaining it, and conducting design reviews to ensure its effectiveness. Ensuring components meet accessibility requirements.
